# Product Marketing Context

*Last updated: 2026-04-26*

## Product Overview
**One-liner:** Pushback is the professional toolkit for freelancers who know their worth — 21 ready-to-use defense tools covering every client situation where a line gets crossed.

**What it does:** Pushback gives freelancers a prepared, professional response to every difficult client situation — scope creep, late payments, kill fees, IP disputes, chargeback threats, and more. A freelancer selects their situation (or pastes a raw client message for AI classification), reviews their position, and gets a copy-ready message in under 30 seconds. It also analyzes uploaded contracts for risk clauses and can generate structured documents (SOW amendments, dispute packages, kill fee invoices) for Pro users.

**Product category:** Freelancer business toolkit / client management software. Customers searching for: "how to respond to scope creep email," "freelance contract protection," "late payment email templates," "freelance client dispute response."

**Product type:** SaaS subscription with a free-to-start tier.

**Business model:** Freemium subscription. Free tier: 1 defense response + 1 contract analysis (no card required). Pro: €20/month — 150 defense responses/month, 50 contract analyses/month, full history, all 21 tools, document generation, client notes, analytics. Billed via Stripe. Prices shown excl. VAT.

## Target Audience
**Target companies:** Independent freelancers — solo operators, not agencies. No company size filter applies.

**Decision-makers:** The freelancer themselves is the buyer, user, and decision-maker. No procurement cycle.

**Primary use case:** A freelancer is in an uncomfortable or adversarial client situation and needs a professional, confident response fast — without sounding emotional, unprepared, or like they don't know their rights.

**Jobs to be done:**
- "Help me respond to this client without sounding angry or unprofessional"
- "Tell me if my contract actually protects me before I push back"
- "Give me the exact words to say so I don't lose money to scope creep or a late payment"

**Use cases:**
- Client asking for free revisions after the agreed limit
- Invoice 2–3 weeks overdue with no response
- Client approved the design, now rejecting the final result as "wrong"
- Client cancelling mid-project and refusing to pay a kill fee
- Client threatening a bad review to extract free work
- Client claiming IP over tools or files not included in the agreement
- Rush delivery demand with no rush fee offered
- Post-delivery discount demand ("it took less time than expected")
- Ghost client blocking project progress
- Chargeback threat after delivery

**Supported freelancer professions (from onboarding):**
- Software / Web Development
- Design (UI/UX, graphic, brand)
- Architecture / Engineering
- Photography / Video
- Writing / Content / Copywriting
- Marketing / Social Media
- Consulting / Strategy
- Music / Audio

## Problems & Pain Points
**Core problem:** Freelancers lose money and projects to difficult clients — not because they don't know their rights, but because they don't know what to say, hesitate too long, or respond emotionally instead of professionally.

**Why alternatives fall short:**
- Generic email templates (Google, Reddit) are too generic, not contract-aware, and require heavy editing under pressure
- FreshBooks / invoice tools handle payment tracking but not conflict response or contract protection
- ChatGPT / Claude prompts require the freelancer to know what to ask — they don't work when you don't know what type of situation you're in
- Legal services / lawyers are expensive, slow, and overkill for everyday scope creep or a late invoice
- Doing nothing / winging it costs money — scope creep alone can wipe project margin entirely

**What it costs them:**
- Lost income: unpaid invoices, unreimbursed scope additions, post-delivery discount demands
- Lost time: multiple follow-up emails, renegotiations, disputes that drag for weeks
- Lost projects: kill fees not enforced, clients walking without paying
- Stress: wasted mental energy on how to phrase difficult conversations

**Emotional tension:**
- "I know I'm right but I don't want to sound aggressive"
- "I don't want to lose the client, but I'm being taken advantage of"
- "I should have caught this in the contract but I didn't"
- "Every time I let this slide, it gets worse next time"

## Competitive Landscape

**Direct:** No known direct competitor with a dedicated 21-tool freelancer conflict response system as of 2026. This specific niche is unaddressed.

**Secondary:**
- **Generic AI (ChatGPT, Claude)** — falls short because: requires the user to know what to ask, produces inconsistent quality, not contract-aware, no freelancer-specific framing or urgency calibration
- **Bonsai / HoneyBook / Dubsado** — falls short because: project management + contract signing tools, not conflict response; help prevent problems but not solve them when they happen
- **FreshBooks / QuickBooks** — falls short because: invoice/payment tracking only, no response guidance, no conflict support
- **Reddit / freelancer communities** — falls short because: slow, inconsistent advice, requires posting and waiting, emotional crowd responses rather than professional framing

**Indirect:**
- **Doing nothing / winging it** — freelancer drafts their own email under pressure; often too soft, too aggressive, or too slow
- **Hiring a lawyer / sending a formal legal letter** — expensive, slow, damages the client relationship, overkill for 80% of situations

## Differentiation

**Key differentiators:**
- 21 pre-built tools for every real freelance conflict situation — not generic writing help
- AI situation classifier: paste a raw client message, get the right tool pre-selected automatically
- Contract risk analysis with clause-level flagging — knows what you signed, not just what you said
- Contract-aware response generation — references actual contract terms, not generic advice
- Document generation for complex situations (SOW amendments, dispute packages, kill fee invoices)
- Payment tracking with overdue detection and one-click late payment tool pre-fill
- Client risk scoring (roadmap): behavioral patterns surfaced from conflict history
- Zero contract storage — contract text is read, analyzed, and deleted (privacy-first)
- Under 30 seconds from situation to copy-ready message

**How we do it differently:** Pushback is a pre-built professional operating system for client conflicts — not a blank prompt, not a template library. Every tool is calibrated to a specific situation, urgency level, and professional tone. Contract analysis is integrated into response generation, not siloed.

**Why that's better:** A freelancer under pressure doesn't need to be a good prompter. They need the right words, right now. Pushback removes the gap between knowing what needs to be said and being able to say it professionally.

**Why customers choose us over alternatives:**
- Speed: faster than drafting, faster than searching Reddit, faster than asking a lawyer
- Confidence: professionally calibrated tone — firm without being aggressive
- Specificity: contract-aware responses, not generic advice
- Coverage: 21 tools means every situation is handled, not just the most common ones
- Freemium entry: try it before any commitment — no card required

## Switching Dynamics

**Push:** Freelancer just got burned — client walked without paying, scope exploded with no extra compensation, or they wasted an afternoon drafting emails that didn't land. The pain is acute and recent.

**Pull:** The promise of having a professional response ready in 30 seconds, specifically for the situation they're in right now. The demo on the landing page shows exactly how it works before any signup friction.

**Habit:** Freelancers are used to either winging it or defaulting to Reddit/Google — both feel "free" even though they have real costs (time, outcome quality, emotional energy).

**Anxiety:** "Will this sound like me?" (response personalization concern) / "Is the AI going to give me generic advice?" / "What happens to my contract PDF after I upload it?" (privacy concern — addressed by zero-storage policy).

## Brand Voice

**Tone:** Direct, serious, confident. Not warm-and-fuzzy. Not aggressive. The tone of a smart lawyer friend who tells you exactly what to do without sugarcoating it.

**Style:** Short sentences. No filler. Action-oriented. Technical precision where it matters (contract clauses, payment terms). Headlines that state the situation as a fact, not a question.

**Personality:**
- Authoritative — knows the territory; doesn't hedge
- Pragmatic — focused on what to do, not how to feel about it
- Protective — squarely on the freelancer's side
- Unflinching — doesn't soften difficult truths about client behavior
- Professional — not edgy, not startup-casual; toolkit, not app
